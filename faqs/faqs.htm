Q:

As a realtor, why do I need a professional stager?

A:

Homes ready for the market should be shown at their peak condition. As an agent, your singular focus is selling the home. As a stager, the focus is getting the home in prime condition for future buyers to see the potential. There may be "issues" with the home, seen only by the stager, that can and should be corrected, which would be a deal breaker for potential buyers if left undone. One would be surprised at how seemingly small annoying details can affect a buyer's opinion of the home. Quite often these issues are addressed with some attention and very little expense but should be taken care of before the home is on the market. Additionally, having a neutral third party maintains the relationship between home owner and agent and results in a beautifully staged home!

Q:

What types of homes should be staged?

A:

Every single home, weather a large estate or smaller property, will benefit from staging. Cleaning areas and addressing spots of concern, getting the home photo-ready can improve the chances for a quick sale for the owner, and present the home in its best light.

Q:

What time commitment is required for a home to be staged?

A:

The time required to stage a home is determined by several things including; the condition of the home, the rooms to be staged, the size of the home, the target audience. An initial walk through and evaluation, with the goals in mind, will determine the staging strategy. Working directly with you and discussing problem areas or rooms that need improving will take place during the initial consultation. Your style preferences, budget requirements and overall finished look will be planned. From that discussion we can do more things in depth or tackle fewer issues- your choice.
